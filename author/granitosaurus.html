Can Ratty Eat it?

A curated list of what can and cannot your pet ratty eat.
Tags General Contribute

Apple

Good and healthy, watch out for seeds.

Apricot

Nutritious, avoid bone.

Avocado

Good in small doses, fatty, skin is poisonous.

Bananas

Good except for green.

Beat tops

Toxic, high levels of oxalates.

Blackbarries

Good, colors the ratty and surroundings.

Blue cheese

The blue mold used in cheese is toxic for ratties.

Boysenberries

Good, colors the ratty and surroundings.

Bread

Good, not much nutritional value.

Broccoli

Healthy in every form, but better cooked.

Brussel sprouts

Only cooked is okay.

Caffeine

Unhealthy even in small doses.

Carbonated drinks

Rats can't burp. usually high in sugar

Carrot

Healthy in almost every form.

Celery

Healthy in almost every form.

Cereal

Usually contains lots of sugars and little nutrients

Cheese

Very fatty, low nutritional value.

Chicken

Healthiest meat for rats.

Corn

Okay when cooked.

Cucumber

Good and healthy.

Egg

Hard boiled

Grapes

Healthy; might prevent cancer; high in natural sugars.

Green beans

Cooked without spices.

Kiwi

Good and healthy in moderation.

Lemon

Very bad, kills the ratty

Licorice

Has neurotoxins.

Mango

Can causes liver cancer.

Milk

Fine in small amounts

Onion

Can lead to anemia and upsets tummy.

Orange

The white part is harmful for male ratties.

Papaya

Good and healthy.

Peach

Good and healthy, beware of stone.

Peanuts

Raw are toxic, fried are okay.

Pear

Good and healthy, watch out for seeds.

Peas

Good and healthy.

Plum

Good and healthy, beware of stone.

Pomegranates

Good and healthy in moderation, messy.

Poppey seeds

Makes ratty lethargic.

Potato

Boiled or mashed

Raspberries

Good, colors the ratty and surroundings.

Raw beans

Destroys vitamin a and digestions enzymes in ratty.

Red cabbage

Only good when cooked.

Rhubarb

High levels of oxalates which bind up calcium.

Romaine lettuce

Healthy and good to go.

Sugar

Unhealthy, makes ratty fat;

Sweet potato

Only good when cooked.

Tofu

Raw is bad but packaged is okay.
This is just a general recommendation based on community experiences. We are not your veterinarian and we don't take responsibility.
